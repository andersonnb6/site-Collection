Apresentation

The LabMicra, which is part of the Universidade Federal do Amazonas (UFAM) Biological Collection, constitutes a scientifically significant asset. LabMicra is affiliated with the World Federation for Culture Collections (WFCC), under registration number WDCM 956. It consists of a collection of Amazonian bacteria, isolated, identified, and preserved using lyophilization and stored at -20ºC.


The bacterial cultures comprising LabMicra are derived from clinical isolates such as those from oropharyngeal and human feces, as well as from natural environments such as river water, streams, and soils. The main bacteria included in the collection are Escherichia coli, Staphylococcus aureus, Bacillus cereus, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, among others. The collection is of significant importance as it is composed of bacterial strains isolated from different substrates of the Brazilian Amazon, a region still underexplored in terms of its microbial richness.


LabMicra provides services to fulfill requests for bacterial culture supply to various national and international research/educational institutions, as well as offering training for students and professionals in bacterial identification. LabMicra is also characterizing the already stored cultures in terms of biotechnological aspects such as the production of industrial enzymes and profiling antibiotic resistance and sensitivity.
